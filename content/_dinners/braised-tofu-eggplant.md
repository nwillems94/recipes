Cut tofu into 1" cubes. Sandwich between several layers of clean kitchen towels and gently press to expel excess liquid. Pat dry.

Heat half the vegetable oil in a large nonstick skillet over medium-high. Cook tofu, stirring and turning occasionally, until golden brown all over, 6–8 minutes. Generously season with kosher salt, then sprinkle evenly with sugar. Cook, stirring constantly, until tofu is starting to brown, about 1 minute. Transfer tofu to a plate, leaving behind as much oil as possible.

Heat remaining oil in same pan. Add eggplant, season with salt, and cook, stirring often, until brown and starting to soften, 5–7 minutes. Add scallions, garlic, ginger, and crushed red pepper flakes. Cook, stirring, until fragrant and starting to soften, about 1 minute. 

Add soy sauce and equal parts water to the pan. Bring to a simmer (it might happen immediately), then reduce heat to low. Cover and cook until eggplant is starting to fall apart, about 2 minutes. 

Return tofu to pan; stir to coat. Remove from heat, stir in rice vinegar and fish sauce.

Serve tofu mixture in a shallow bowl over steamed rice, topped with thinly sliced dark green scallion parts and drizzled with sesame oil.
